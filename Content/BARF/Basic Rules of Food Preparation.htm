Basic Rules For The Preparation of BARF Meals

In order to prevent contamination and risks related to handling of raw meat, you should follow these simple rules to ensure your and your dog's safety.

  • Hygiene first.

    • Remember to wash your hands, kitchen utensils and work surface.

  • Serving.

    • Meat can be cut into pieces, and can also be served ground.

    • Supplements can be sprinkled into the meal or previously mixed with water and added to in this form.

    • Only use meat of quality intended for human consumption!

    • Do not serve bare bones, only bones with meat surrounding them.

    • Do not serve large, weight-bearing bones.

    • Adjust the bones to the size of the dog.

    • Cook the vegetables for a long time, until very soft. Additionally, for better digestibility, you can blend them. Fruit can be served raw.

    • In recipes without bones, you must always use a calcium supplement, e.g. in the form of eggshell flour.

  • Storing prepped meals

    • Meals can be frozen and stored for up to 2 months.

    • Meals should be frozen in packaging designed for this purpose — food containers, food bags, food jars, etc.

    • It is best to defrost meals overnight in the refrigerator.

    • Defrosted meals can be stored in the refrigerator for a maximum of 2-3 days, but the sooner you use them, the better.
