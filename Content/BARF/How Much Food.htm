How Much Food

Generally, you will establish the correct amount based on the body weight of your pet. The consensus is that you should feed about 2 to 3% of the dog's ideal body weight.

Here's how to do it:

  1. Weigh your dog.

  2. Asses your dog's ideal weight:

    1. If your dog has good back hip and muscle definition, with easily palpable ribs — it's in perfect shape.

    2. If your dog has little to no back hip definition and square undercarriage — it's overweight.

    3. If you dog has visible ribs and overly prominent hip bones — it's likely underweight.

  3. Choose a percentage. Add or subtract about 0.5% if your dog is not at its perfect weight. Here's a table according to your dog's activity:

inactive 2%
average 2,5%
active 3%
athlete / working 3,5%

Please note that these percentages do not apply to puppies, senior or sick dogs.

Feeding Times

Let's talk about feeding times:

  • The healthiest way to feed an adult dog is twice a day within a six-hour period.

  • Feed them one larger meal and one smaller meal; this can be breakfast and lunch or lunch and dinner — whatever is more convenient for you.

  • By feeding your dog within a six-hour window, with a few exceptions for light treats, you’ll give their digestive system 18 hours a day to recover.
